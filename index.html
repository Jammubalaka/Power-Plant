Power Generation Types

Conventional Sources:-

THERMAL POWER PLANT

Thermal power generation plant or thermal power station is the most conventional source of electric power.The installed capacity of the thermal power station is 1720 MW.

NUCLEAR POWER PLANT

A nuclear power plant is a thermal power station in which the heat source is a nuclear reactor. As is typical of thermal power stations, heat is used to generate steam that drives a steam turbine connected to a generator that produces electricity.

HYDRO POWER PLANT

Generation of electricity by hydropower (potential energy in stored water) is one of the cleanest methods of producing electric power.In 2012, hydroelectric power plants contributed about 16% of total electricity generation of the world.

Non-Conventional Sources:-

WIND POWER PLANT

Generation of electricity by hydropower (potential energy in stored water) is one of the cleanest methods of producing electric power.In 2015,there were over 200,000 wind turbines operating, with a total nameplate capacity of 432 GW worldwide.

SOLAR POWER PLANT

Solar power is the conversion of energy from sunlight into electricity, either directly using photovoltaics (PV), or a combination. Concentrated solar power systems use lenses or mirrors and solar tracking systems to focus a large area of sunlight into a small beam.

BIO-MASS POWER PLANT

PNG Biomass grows up to 10 million seedlings a year in nurseries in the Markham Valley in Papua New Guinea. They will generate 30 MW of electrical power by growing the seedlings into trees and burning them in a biomass power plant.
